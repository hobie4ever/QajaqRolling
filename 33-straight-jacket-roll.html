33 Straight Jacket Roll

David Täng

Correct, I haven't made it yet. This is what I can tell you about my journey so far.

Prerequisites: 
A strong will to succeed, the process can be a bit painful at times. 
A qajaq that fits you, low back deck, full contact with your thighs. 
Excellent twist in your torso (I'm not there yet)
An understanding of what supports you; water, shoulders, secondary stability, weight distribution inside and out. 
You should be able to do an Elbow roll with some ease, double elbow as well. If not start there. 

Yoga and stretching. YES! For me this is somewhat painful but makes all the difference. And it takes time so don't set a timetable, do what you can and things will come to you. 

Qajaq: This is one of the reasons I got into SOF qajaq building, so I can build myself a qajaq which allows me to do a SJ roll. Not that the qajaq does anything for you but some attributes need to be fulfilled. My current one (TTD6 - TvångsTröja David #6 or Straight Jacket David #6) is great, built for this purpose alone. There are two issues with it, the masik is a tad too high and it's black, l loved my brown ones. The RRD5 (Rockhopping and Roll David#5) had a masik slightly lower and I had to push my knees under it, so I raised it 1 cm for TTD6 and now I regret it a bit. When I lean forward my arms touch the masik stopping me from getting low on the foredeck. This isn't a problem in forward finishing rolls since my arms are not in the way but....

The three main things I work on at the moment are:
Getting my shoulders flat and parallel to the surface as I reach up towards the sky before I start the backward sweep.
Getting my shoulders "wide" at the surface and still keep my elbows to my torso. As I push my shoulders out my hands like to slip out of my armpits and my elbows like to leave my body. It feels like a mental thing, well I'll let you know...
Timing! If I try to recover on the back deck too soon, the qajaq isn't passed the second stability far enough and I simply capsize it back over me. If I recover too late I don't have the righting momentum of the qajaq to pull me up the last bit to get past the tipping point. 

With weight distribution I am thinking about the non-water leg. Should I push it out to the side to get a better weight distribution, counterweight or push it down to get more speed, more momentum in the qajaq? Perhaps it doesn't matter as long as I use it actively?
