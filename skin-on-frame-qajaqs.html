Skin on Frame Qajaqs

Oh how I love the extremely low volume East Greenland qajaqs. Why do I write "qajaq" and not kayak? Well, first because I'm a nerd 游때. Second, as I have grasped the use of the words, a qajaq is a traditional skin on frame kayak even if it hasn't been skinned with sealskin. A kayak can are normally built in other materials such as plastics, carbon fibre etc. A "kayak" with some may call "recreational" isn't really a qajaq - it looks a bit like a kayak but there is a Greenlandic word -"qarariaq" - what I think means "like a qajaq" or "almost a qajaq". To call a kayak a "boat" is not correct in my opinion. Not that it's wrong but a qajaq is a traditional hunting tool, and together with the dogsled are the two most important tools for surviving. So to honour the arctic culture and all the people who died while hunting in a qajaq I hope I do the right thing. Thirdly - it looks beautiful "Qajaq"!
